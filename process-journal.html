PROCESS JOURNAL

Introduction

As the population continues to grow, the demand for ambulance services is predicted to increase in a similar fashion. To keep up with this ever-growing demand, the current ambulance service system must be revised and further assessed. As we slowly start to welcome an autonomous technology driven future, there is potential to improve ambulance services even further by streamlining the procedure and improve response and further benefiting society. This project focuses on exploring how autonomous technologies can be integrated in emergency ambulance services to improve the overall process and experience.

1. Background Research

Figure 1.1 - Sally using an academic database

Literary, interview and survey research regarding the current state of ambulance services was conducted to provide insights into our chosen context and environment. A large collection of responses were received, ranging from various demographics of everyday citizens, to the medical staff directly involved with these services. The aim was to target people's thoughts on past experiences with ambulances, in regards to both the procedure and their overall feelings during their experiences. By analysing the range of different responses, common themes, problems and needs arose which helped narrowed down the area to be focused on and provide a general understanding of the issues that need to be solved.
